**E26 — Donchian Channel (Dennis & Eckhardt 1983):** Breakout signal failed in
the 2000–2009 dot-com fold (Sharpe −0.158); entering on 20-day highs caught the
peak of the tech bubble and couldn't recover without the SMA200 anchor. The
breakout signal is largely absorbed by v2 once prices are above SMA200.

**E27 — 52-Week High (George & Hwang 2004):** Strongest behavioral-finance
candidate. DD improvement (−19.75% vs v2 −20.5%) confirms theory has merit at
index level, but **OOS correlation to v2 = 0.937** — near-duplicate. When SPY
is near its 52-week high, it is almost always also above its SMA200. The two
signals carry almost identical information. Mean WF dragged by fold-1 (0.386).
DSR 0.945 < 0.95 bar. Closed.

**E28 — ADX Trend Strength (Wilder 1978):** Most promising of the three on
risk metrics. ADX(p14,t25) produced the **second-lowest DD ever tested at
−9.65%** (after CTA at −6.19%) and OOS Sharpe 1.003. However, fold-2
(2010–2019) Sharpe collapsed to 0.463 — the bull-market upswing produced
erratic ADX levels that generated choppy in/out signals. Best balanced
config ADX(p14,t20): mean_wf 0.797 (< 0.844 bar), DSR 0.856 (< 0.95 bar).
Not watch-list eligible on any config.

**Key cross-experiment insight:** All three strategies confirm that the SMA200
level gate in v2 already captures the core signal these philosophies encode.
The 52-week high *is* proximity to SMA200 in disguise (corr 0.937). The
Donchian channel on a single asset produces signal already embedded in v2.
The ADX adds marginal information but at the cost of fold stability. v2 remains
the most parsimonious, stable signal found in 192 configs.
