## RESEARCH — Configs 174→180

**E25 Vigilant Asset Allocation (VAA, 6 configs, pre-reg run_2026_07_21.py)**

_Philosophy_: Keller & Keuning (2017). Score = 12×M1 + 4×M3 + 2×M6 + 1×M12 for each asset. If fraction of offensive assets scoring ≤ 0 ≥ breadth_prot: rotate 100% to the highest-scoring defensive asset (from SHY/IEF/TLT/GLD). Else: go 100% to the best offensive asset (from SPY/QQQ/IWM). Monthly rebalance. The "competitive defensive selection" was designed to avoid the 2022 bond-carnage problem that killed Dual Momentum (E3/E7 — TLT was the only defensive option; here GLD or SHY auto-wins when bonds fall).

**VAA family CLOSED.** Root cause: the Keller & Keuning paper tested 12 offensive assets — at that scale "any-1-of-12 negative" is a meaningful breadth signal. At 3 assets, "2-of-3 must fail" is too blunt. Fold-2 (2010-2019 bull market) Sharpe 0.409 reveals the core problem: concentrated single-asset monthly rotation misses momentum within a narrow equity universe. The n=4 configs (with EFA) are even worse — reproducing E9 GEM's finding that international equity drag kills fold-2 returns.

Interesting contradiction: VAA's terminal value ($9,202) beats v2 ($8,382) on raw return. This is driven by fold-3 OOS Sharpe 0.981 vs v2's 0.893. VAA captures the 2020-2025 bull run well (single asset concentration works in a trending market). But fold-1 and fold-2 expose the strategy's fragility, making the risk-adjusted profile inferior.
