## What was researched
Trend-following via long-term moving averages (research queue item 1):
Faber (2007) 10-month SMA timing; Zakamulin (2014, 2018) on realistic costs,
out-of-sample decay and data-mining bias. Notes: research/2026-07-13-ma-trend-following.md.

## What was built (first session — includes scaffold)
- Project scaffold: strategies/, backtest/ (backtesting.py engine), data/
  (loader + committed snapshot), research/, reports/, tests/.
- Data: sandbox blocks every market-data API; only github.com is reachable.
  Adopted SteelCerberus/us-market-data (daily SPY total-return proxy,
  1885 -> 2025-12-19). **DIA/QQQ/Mag-7 benchmarks unavailable until the
  multi-asset pipeline lands (roadmap #1).**
- strategies/sma_trend.py — long when Close > (1+b)·SMA(w), flat when
  Close < (1−b)·SMA(w), else hold. 2 params (w, b). 0.1%/side costs, cash at 0%.
- tests/test_no_lookahead.py — proves signal causality (future truncation
  invariance), t+1 execution, and that costs are actually charged.

## Keep/revert decision
KEEP. Rule: OOS Sharpe must beat champion (none yet -> SPY B&H 0.774; got 0.871)
and MaxDD must not worsen >10% relative (improved: -20.8% vs -33.7%). Exactly the
Zakamulin pattern: lags B&H in the bull IS decade, pays for itself in 2020/2022.
Honest caveats: single-asset, close-only data ending 2025-12-19; cash earns 0%;
T12M headline *underperforms* B&H in dollars ($1,089 vs $1,156) — the win is risk.
