**E29 — VIX-Regime Dynamic Ensemble (Ang & Bekaert 2002 / Whaley 2009):**
Three-regime system switching between pure v2 (VIX<20), v2+Bollinger blend (VIX 20–30),
and CTA multi-asset (VIX>30). Mean WF 0.825 (< 0.844 bar), DSR 0.972 (✓), but
**CI [−0.589,+0.927] straddles zero** and OOS correlation to v2 = 0.967 — the highest
correlation ever recorded. Root cause: crisis periods (VIX>30) occur only ~10% of
trading days; the CTA-SPY sleeve during normal/elevated periods replicates the same
SMA200 gate logic that drives v2. The `RE(v20/c30,v2only)` config (no elevated/crisis
blending, pure regime indicator) scored mean_wf 0.814 and end$1k $7,548 — essentially
v2 with worse CI, confirming the overlay adds nothing. Family **PERMANENTLY CLOSED**
(not watch-list eligible: CI fails AND corr 0.967 >> 0.70 ensemble threshold).

**E30 — Inter-Market Bond-Equity Relative Strength (Murphy 1991 / Faber 2007):**
SPY vs IEF 3-month momentum as a risk-off scaling overlay on v2. When IEF beats SPY
over the lookback, v2 exposure is multiplied by `reduced_scale` (0.5 default). Best
IM(lb63, scale0.50): mean WF 0.769 (< 0.844 bar), DSR 0.947 (< 0.95 bar), **CI straddles
zero**, corr_v2 = 0.974. Key insight: when IEF outperforms SPY over 63 days, SPY has
usually been falling — the v2 SMA200 gate has **already** signaled cash or reduced
exposure. The inter-market filter adds extra caution after v2 already captured the
regime shift. Zero-exposure variant (scale=0.00) proved the point: mean_wf collapsed
to 0.601 with DD −24.42%. Family **PERMANENTLY CLOSED** (no config passes the bar;
the signal is structurally absorbed by v2).

**Cross-experiment insight (sessions 13–20, 30 experiments):** At the broad SPY index
level, the SMA200 + vol-targeting framework (v2) captures essentially all of the
practical regime information available in equity prices. Overlaying VIX regimes,
bond-equity relative strength, ADX trend strength, or 52-week-high proximity adds
redundant information already encoded in the SMA200 gate. v2 is not just a champion;
it is the most parsimonious, consistent encoding of the equity trend signal on a single
broad index. The research agenda must look beyond single-index SPY overlays.
